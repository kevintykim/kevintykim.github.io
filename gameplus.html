Project Overview
Design an online marketplace app for retro video games.


Role
UI Designer

Tools
Adobe XD

Duration
1 month



The 5 W’s

“Design a mobile app, web app, and/or responsive website for an online store. This store is for users who want to buy goods from home or on the go. The product can be used anytime and anywhere users can connect to the Internet.”

After I read the project brief, the idea to sell retro video games came easily to me. Many physical retailers’ game inventories are limited to only the latest titles, while online retailers offer a wider range of games but carry a lower guarantee of quality. GamePlus was conceived as a specialized store that produces top-condition copies of older games.



Brand Guidelines

The GamePlus brand was crafted to exude both modern sleekness and retro charm. The guiding principles emphasize ease and convenience, the combination of colors and typefaces is clean yet vibrant, and the icons evoke a comforting nostalgia.



User Flow

To decide the essential features, I drew on my own experiences with shopping apps and narrowed down three user stories to use as the basis for GamePlus. To paint a clear picture of the journey for myself, each story was first mapped out individually.

USER STORY #1: “I want to place multiple items in a shopping cart, so I can purchase more than one item at a time.”

USER STORY #2: “I want advanced filtering options, so I don’t have to browse through a lot of products to find what I’m looking for.”

USER STORY #3: “I want to see an overview of my previous activity, so I can view previous purchases and search history.”

Low/Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity sketches were roughly based on common shopping app layouts, in order to avoid alienating new users and keep the experience engagingly familiar.

This phase laid the groundwork to build a functional, intuitive set of screens. Using templates that have worked successfully in other apps (such as Amazon and eBay) helped the wireframes reach a base standard for user-centered design.



Usability Test Results

Four participants tested a prototype of the app’s search function, with the following scenario: “You’re looking for Super Bowling, a Sports game for the Nintendo 64.”



High-Fidelity Mockups

The final designs updated the UI with a playfully retro aesthetic. The familiar layout templates used in the low-fidelity stage also upheld the app’s commitment to a clear, convenient user experience.

The search filter popup was redesigned to maximize ease-of-use, based on the usability test results.

The History function was designed similarly to the search filter popup, providing greater visual cohesion to the app overall.



Retrospective

The brand guidelines phase inspired the most creativity as I drafted various ways of building a simple and clean atmosphere, with enough aesthetic quirkiness to keep the UI from feeling generic. And since the branding came at the beginning of this particular project, I could approach it as a blank state, unconstrained by previously made wireframes or user flows.

One major takeaway was the importance of usability testing to iron out pain points. The search filter was especially challenging, as test feedback showed that this feature needed a more minimal, stripped-back design before people could understand and comfortably use it—a problem I was not aware of until receiving outsider input.

For the future, I am keen to revisit and expand this project with full sets for the tablet and desktop designs. As it stands, I focused primarily on building GamePlus as a mobile app, but translating all of the screens to other viewports would be a great way to both supplement the project and grow my design proficiency.
