Objective: ‘80s Reverb is a music player that takes listeners back to the 1980s. Users can create profiles, search for artists, and make playlists, among other functions.


Context: The founders of a brand new ‘80s music player would like you to define the UI direction that will work for their branding and digital UI.


Role: UI Designer


Tools: Adobe XD


Duration: 1 month




Competitive Analysis

First comes ideation on how to implement the app’s various functions. Similar apps are studied to gather inspiration for this process.



Low-Fidelity Wireframes & User Flow

Next, early versions of the app screens are represented on paper. The sketches are also arranged into a user flow diagram that maps out the journey through ‘80s Reverb.



Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

The rough paper designs are translated into the digital realm. The refinement process begins.



Icon Set

Many potential icons are brainstormed. Once chosen, the proper icons will further add to the app design’s overall polish.



Mood Board

Brainstorming also brings forth a mood board that embodies the app’s overall tone and personality. The chosen colors, textures, and imagery will go a long way in developing a distinct look and feel for ‘80s Reverb.



Final Mockups

The icons and mood board have been integrated with the earlier wireframes. ‘80s Reverb has fully taken shape and is ready for handoff to developers!



Retrospective

‘80s Reverb was an exercise not only in creating a UI, but in ideating several key functionalities, such as searching for artists and making music playlists. From the start, I wanted to give the app a vibrant yet calming atmosphere, evocative of 1980s nightlife, and chose my color palette and other elements accordingly.

I look back on this project with great appreciation for my designs, my features, my learnings, and the general work that goes into UI design. I also came away eager to experiment with UI styles in the future. If I ever revisit the project or design a similar app, I would change aspects such as the icons, the color palette, and the grid layouts, in order to see if I can take different approaches to achieve the same overall tone.

This was also my first time delving into Adobe XD, and I used this opportunity to grow my skills with the software. Some parts of the design process were more enjoyable than others. For example, the icon set provided a surprisingly fun creative outlet for me, while the mood board required greater thought and concentration. However, I overcame any challenges by stepping back to engage with the material more slowly and patiently. ‘80s Reverb was a learning experience as well as a design project, and in the end it was valuable in both respects.
