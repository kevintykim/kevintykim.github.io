Objective: A specialized online marketplace for older video games that aren’t found at general retailers. Shop GamePlus on your phone, tablet, or desktop.


Role: UI Designer


Tools: Adobe XD


Duration: 1 month



Brand Guidelines

The GamePlus brand was crafted to exude both modern sleekness and retro charm.
The guiding principles emphasize ease and convenience for users. The icons, reminiscent of early 8-bit game sprites, evoke a comforting nostalgia. The stark color palette and Assistant typeface establish a modern style, while the more vibrant accent colors and FFF Forward font add life to the UI.



User Flow

Three user stories took the main focus, based on common tasks and functions for shopping apps:
• “Put items in a shopping cart, so I can buy multiple items at a time”
• “Advanced filtering options, so I don’t have to look through too many products”
• “Overview of previous activity, so I can see previous purchases and search history”

To make the journey clear and easy for users, the path from “Sign in” to each endpoint was created individually, before all three were merged into the overarching map above.



Low/Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity sketches were roughly based on common layouts, again taking inspiration from other shopping apps. This was crucial for GamePlus, as the starting templates would keep the experience engagingly familiar and avoid alienating new users.



Usability Test Results

Four participants tested a prototype of the app’s search function, with the following scenario: “You’re looking for Super Bowling, a Sports game for the Nintendo 64.” The testers’ feedback exposed several pain points, the greatest one being a violation of Hick’s law.



Final Mockups

The final designs updated the UI with a quirky, retro aesthetic. The familiar layout templates used in the low-fidelity stage also upheld the app’s commitment to a clear, convenient user experience.
The most notable change implemented after the usability test was the search filter popup, which was redesigned to maximize ease-of-use. A similar design was used for the app’s History function as well.
