Objective: An online marketplace for retro video games that aren’t found at general retailers. Shop GamePlus on your phone, tablet, or desktop.


Role: UI Designer


Tools: Adobe XD


Duration: 1 month



The 5 W’s

The project brief outlined the following framework, allowing the freedom to choose a distinct store type:
• WHAT: An online store, designed as a responsive website, web app, and/or mobile app.
• WHO: Users who want to buy goods without going to a physical store.
• WHY: People would rather shop from home or on the go.
• WHEN: Anytime.
• WHERE: Anywhere the user can connect to the Internet.



Brand Guidelines

The GamePlus brand was crafted to exude both modern sleekness and retro charm. The guiding principles emphasize ease and convenience, the combination of colors and typefaces is clean yet vibrant, and the icons evoke a comforting nostalgia.



User Flow

Three user stories took the spotlight:
• “I want to place multiple items in a shopping cart, so I can purchase more than one item at a time.”
• “I want advanced filtering options, so I don’t have to browse through a lot of products to find what I’m looking for.”
• “I want to see an overview of my previous activity, so I can view previous purchases and search history.”

To make the journey clear and easy for users, the path from “Sign in” to each endpoint was created individually, before all three were merged into the overarching map above.



Low/Mid-Fidelity Wireframes

The low-fidelity sketches were roughly based on common shopping app layouts, in order to avoid alienating new users and keep the experience engagingly familiar.



Usability Test Results

Four participants tested a prototype of the app’s search function, with the following scenario: “You’re looking for Super Bowling, a Sports game for the Nintendo 64.”



Final Mockups

The final designs updated the UI with a playfully retro aesthetic. The familiar layout templates used in the low-fidelity stage also upheld the app’s commitment to a clear, convenient user experience.
The most notable change implemented after the usability test was the search filter popup, which was redesigned to maximize ease-of-use. A similar design was used for the app’s History function as well.



Retrospective

The idea for a game store came easily to me, as my own interest in gaming makes me the ideal customer for an app like GamePlus. Many physical retailers’ game inventories are limited to only the latest titles, while online retailers, despite offering a wider range of games, suffer from a much lower guarantee of quality. GamePlus was conceived as a specialized store that produces top-condition copies of older games.

The brand guidelines phase inspired the most creativity as I drafted various ways of building a simple and clean atmosphere, with enough aesthetic quirkiness to keep the UI from feeling generic. And since the branding came at the beginning of this particular project, I could approach it as a blank state, unconstrained by previously made wireframes or user flows.

One major takeaway was the importance of usability testing to iron out pain points. The search filter was especially challenging, as test feedback showed that this feature needed a more minimal, stripped-back design before people could understand and comfortably use it—a problem I was not aware of until receiving outsider input.

For the future, I’m also keen to revisit and expand this project with full sets for the tablet and desktop designs. As it stands, I focused primarily on building GamePlus as a mobile app, but translating all of the screens to other viewports would be a great way to both supplement the project and grow my design proficiency.
